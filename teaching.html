Overview

Teaching philosophy, footprint, and the learning experience I aim to create.

Teaching at KFUPM (Since 2004)

I have taught graduate and undergraduate courses at KFUPM since 2004—covering core curriculum, electives, professional masters offerings, and research supervision. I taught 30+ courses and actively contributed to course development, improvement, and coordination.

  • AI/ML, cybersecurity, networking, systems, and software engineering
  • Active learning and inquiry-based learning (IBL) where appropriate
  • Course websites (WebCT/Blackboard) and curated resource pages

How Students Experience My Courses

I focus on clarity, fairness, and deep understanding—using structured learning progressions and authentic tasks that mirror real professional practice.

  • Clear outcomes, transparent rubrics, and timely feedback
  • Hands-on labs, guided practice, and project-based evaluation
  • Curated links and tools that support independent learning
  • Supportive environment with high standards

Signature Teaching Themes

  • Security & Trust: information assurance, network security, cryptography
  • AI with Discipline: sound evaluation, responsible practice, deployment awareness
  • Networks & Systems Thinking: protocols, analysis, design trade-offs
  • Learning at Scale: multi-section coordination with consistent quality
