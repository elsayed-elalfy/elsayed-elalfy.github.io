Short Bio

El-Sayed M. El-Alfy is a Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM), Saudi Arabia, with over three decades of academic and professional experience spanning both industry and academia. His work focuses on trustworthy and deployable artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and intelligent secure systems, with emphasis on privacy-preserving and real-world AI solutions.

He has held research affiliations with the SDAIA–KFUPM Joint Research Center for Artificial Intelligence and the Interdisciplinary Research Center for Intelligent Secure Systems. His research portfolio spans nature-inspired computation, federated and multimodal deep learning, computer vision, biometrics, data analytics, cloud–edge intelligence, ethical and sustainable AI, and intelligent secure system applications.

Prof. El-Alfy has authored numerous peer-reviewed publications, books, and patents, and has delivered keynote and invited lectures at international venues. He serves in editorial leadership roles, including Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of Data, Information and Management (Springer), and has contributed to leading journals such as Artificial Intelligence Review, IEEE/CAA Journal of Automatica Sinica, and IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems.

A Senior Member of IEEE and an approved ABET/CSAB Program Evaluator and NCAAA Panel Reviewer, he actively contributes to academic governance, accreditation, curriculum design, and quality assurance in higher education. He has been recognized among the top 2% of scientists worldwide (Stanford University ranking) and has received multiple distinctions, including the Excellence in Teaching Award from KFUPM and King Abdullah's Award for Scientific Book Translation.

His sustained scholarly impact, interdisciplinary collaborations, and leadership in research and higher education have established him as a respected researcher, mentor, and academic leader in artificial intelligence and intelligent secure systems.
